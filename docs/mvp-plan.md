# TaoTrails V1 MVP Plan

## Positioning

TaoTrails should not start as a full Taoism encyclopedia. V1 should be a focused bridge between English cultural curiosity and China travel intent:

> Discover Taoist wisdom, sacred mountains, and practical temple etiquette for a thoughtful journey through China.

It should connect to the China travel series as a specialized vertical. TaoTrails owns culture-first search intent; the China travel planner owns itinerary, arrival, payment, and transport intent.

## V1 Page Set

- `/` - brand home, sacred map preview, cluster entry points.
- `/taoism/` - core culture hub.
- `/sacred-mountains/` - travel-intent hub.
- `/sacred-mountains/wudang-mountain/` - first destination test page.
- `/sacred-mountains/zhongnan-mountain/` - hermit and Louguantai test page.
- `/lifestyle/temple-etiquette/` - practical high-intent guide with checklist.
- `/lifestyle/taoist-tea/` - tea culture page with brewing timer.
- `/about/` - editorial standards, disclaimers, and relationship to the travel planner.

## Optimization Notes

1. Keep medical and health claims out of V1. Qigong, fasting, Taoist medicine, and internal alchemy should be education-only and carry disclaimers when added later.
2. Avoid changing ticket, opening-hour, visa, and transport details unless they can be freshly verified. Use "verify before travel" language for changing facts.
3. Do not overbuild automation before traffic exists. Add Search Console, analytics, sitemap, and link checking first.
4. Keep the China travel relationship contextual: "planning your arrival in China" links belong near mountain guides and practical tools, not every paragraph.
5. Use tools where they fit the reader's task: temple checklist and tea timer are better V1 retention tools than a complex map backed by unverified route data.

## 30-Day Success Signals

- Core pages indexed.
- Search Console impressions for Taoism basics, Wudang, Zhongnan, temple etiquette, or Taoist tea.
- Clicks from TaoTrails to the China travel planner CTA.
- Strong scroll depth or repeated interaction with checklist/timer pages.
